03/27/24 Phase 2 Retrospective

I have to be honest, as I said in my last post i'm coming to terms with thhe fact that I may not be a good programmer but i'm a programmer none the less. As I went back to study for my assesment I was watching a bunch of videos, and taking extra courses and I realized that the things that held me back in math are holding me back with programming. The logic it takes to work with code is very similar to the logic of an algebra, trigonometry, geometry and etc. And I failed all of those classes in High school, but it's only because I never gave it a fair effort. I usually lean into fields where I have an advantage and I'm realizing that I have to put in the work of expanding my logic to get better. I'm not going to be the best programmer but I can be a good one. I'm going to keep pushing forward and I'm going to keep learning. I started a book called "the mind for numbers" which goes hand in hand with a coursera course that teaches you how to learn. But the book specifically is meant to help people understand complex math logic. So as i'm wrapping up the second phase as an honestly below average react progammer, I'm looking forward to more learning opportunities, and just simply having the time to grow.
